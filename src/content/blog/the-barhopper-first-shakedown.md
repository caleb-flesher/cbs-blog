# The Barhopper's First Official Shakedown - Thoughts

## March 23rd, 2026

I’ve done the first official shakedown of The Barhopper - and it’s completely blown me away.

I rode it one time around the BWI loop, totaling somewhere between 10.5 and 12 miles, and averaged 13 mph! Strava claims it took me 48 minutes to complete the ride.

Firstly, I want to say I am so thankful for the support I had during the build of the bike. I truly feel that all the support I received translated into the best possible bike I could have built, and this ride has proved that to me. This bike is FAST. I don’t mean in a “oh yeah, it’s quickish” sort of way, I mean in the “it’s way faster than it should be” way. It just feels light and agile, that it can climb up anything either at a leisurely pace or hammer down. I don’t intend to brag - but it was passing e-bikes on some climbs and was able to keep up with some hybrid and road bikes for a bit.

Even with the knobby tires, it really just wants to get up and go. A quick shift down, pedal, and shift up will have you at full sprint in no time. I also love how snappy the steering is. The new headset bearings certainly help, but I think the Cruise Control bars have a strong feel with decent feedback, as well as solid comfort. I really love how they look on the bike, but truly they serve a purpose as well. Even though the brakes are a bit on the cheaper side (the levers, specifically), they do have a good feel and are comfortable with plenty of response.

Lastly - the gearing. Wow! I took a wild guess on what size chain ring to swap to and it is mind blowing just how well ratioed it feels. On a single track climb, it may leave a bit to be desired but for road riding at least, it feels well proportioned. It makes me excited to pit this thing further against it’s limits to see just how capable it can be.

So excited to see more of just what The Barhopper can take!
